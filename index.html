Capacities of Pipes, Tanks and Cylinders

Formulas and Equations for determining the capacities:

• D²″ × L″ × 0.0034 = Capacity (gallons)
* Constant 0.0034 = the amount of water in a 1″ pipe, 1″ long. • D²″ × L′ × 0.0408 = Capacity (gallons)
* Constant 0.0408 = the amount of water in a 1″ pipe, 1′ long. • D²′ × L′ × 5.875 = Capacity (gallons)
* Constant 5.875 = the amount of water in a 1′ pipe, 1′ long.

Formula/Equation for Determining the LENGTH of a-(Pipe, Tank or Cylinder) =
Length = Capacity ÷ (D²″ × 0.0408)

Formula/Equation for Determining the DIAMETER of a-(Pipe, Tank or Cylinder)
Diameter = √(Capacity ÷ (L′ × 0.0408))
